The "First Mover" Advantage

Our strategy is structured around the supply deficit window. Selling when the market is empty, before the northern bulk harvest arrives.

Accelerated Market Entry

The northern regions (Quba-Khachmaz) are the main producers, but they harvest 2-3 weeks later. We enter the market in May-June when supply is structurally low and prices are at their peak (60-80% premium). By the time the bulk supply arrives, we have already sold our crop.

900+ Chill Hours

Unlike lowland zones (Kurdamir), our foothills guarantee trees a full winter dormancy. This is a critical factor for consistent flowering and crop formation.

UV Impact & Coloration

Elevation provides 15% more UV radiation. Cherries achieve a premium "Dark" color, and Giant Red Apricots become deep red. The visual appeal drives export demand.

Calcium Structure

Carbonate soils act as natural cement for the fruit. The texture becomes dense and crunchy, allowing safe long-haul transport.

No Logistics Burden

Sales are executed through pre-qualified export partners under contractual purchase arrangements (FCA Model). Buyers handle packing and transport. Pre-payment on the field.

Realistic Biological Timeline

Planting in Q4 2026. Rooting in 2027. First commercial harvest in 2029 (Year 3). This conservative modeling eliminates the risk of unmet expectations.
